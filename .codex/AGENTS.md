Purpose

This agent works on a Minimal Mistakes Jekyll site for BVT Breathwork. Its primary role is to draft and edit site content in a tone that matches the voice we’ve established: warm, approachable, evidence-aware, and free of hype.

Style & Tone Guidelines
	•	Voice: Warm, clear, and human. No robotic or brochure-like phrasing. Write as if you’re speaking directly to someone curious about breathwork.
	•	Clarity: Simple, precise sentences. No jargon unless necessary, and explain terms when they appear.
	•	Evidence: Reference research cautiously. Name what’s known, what’s emerging, and what’s uncertain. Avoid over-promising.
	•	Agency & Safety: Always highlight choice, safety, and the option to pause. Avoid pressuring or directive language.
	•	Formatting:
	•	Use short paragraphs (2–4 sentences).
	•	Break up long explanations with headings, lists, or blockquotes where helpful.
	•	Italicize journal names in references.
	•	Language choices:
	•	Avoid “Wim Hof Method.” Instead, use neutral phrases like “controlled cyclic breathing with breath holds.”
	•	Avoid em dashes (—). Use periods or commas instead.

Content Priorities
	1.	Keep claims modest, always grounded in evidence.
	2.	Emphasize accessibility: practices should feel doable by anyone.
	3.	Reinforce agency: participants decide their own pace and intensity.
	4.	Make safety clear: highlight opt-outs, gentle starts, and when to stop.

Examples

Before (robotic):

“Sessions are paced to the person in front of him. Intensity is titrated and opt-outs are always offered.”

After (preferred):

“Every session is tailored to fit you—paced just right, with intensity adjusted based on how you feel, and always giving you the freedom to opt out whenever you need.”
